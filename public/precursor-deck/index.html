The deeper I went, the clearer it became: this needs to be the independent standard — the WTI for compute — that every institution licenses.

The Problem

No standard. No visibility. No hedge.
Three unsolved problems in the world's fastest-growing commodity market.

No Pricing Standard

  • 250+ providers, ~60 chip architectures — no shared benchmark
  • H100 ≠ MI300X at identical $/hr — apples vs. oranges
  • Training and inference have different cost physics — no index captures both

Degradation Is Invisible

  • GPU performance degrades over chip lifetime — no market standard exists to track it
  • Buyers pay spec-sheet prices for hardware running below spec
  • No market publishes performance-adjusted pricing

Exposure Can't Be Hedged

  • $100B+/yr in compute spend locked into EDPs, MACCs, CUDs
  • Prices move — no futures, no OTC swap, no ETF to offset risk
  • Without a standard index, no instrument can exist

CU Swap — Contract for Differences

Enterprise pays: Fixed CU rate

Counterparty pays: Floating CU Index, monthly

Settlement: Net cash difference — no GPU changes hands

Exit: Sell the position. No penalties. No ratchets.
