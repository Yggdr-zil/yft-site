About
Bear enrolled at Texas Tech to pursue a pharmacology degree, but quickly realized the traditional path wasn't for him. He became obsessed with unconventional methods of creating value — solving hard problems and monetizing the solutions. He and his long-time friend built models to generate alpha in the market, and created a volatility forecaster that landed both of them an internship at a quant firm. That experience cemented his conviction: he wanted to work at the intersection of finance and technology.

While studying compute markets, Bear recognized that GPU compute was following the same trajectory as oil before WTI standardization — fragmented, opaque, and impossible to hedge. He studied how Southwest Airlines survived the 2000s jet fuel crisis through disciplined hedging, how the standardization of crude oil pricing created a trillion-dollar derivatives market, and how the VIX transformed volatility from a risk into a tradeable asset class. He saw the same structural gap in compute: no benchmark, no transparency, no way to hedge. He set out to build the missing infrastructure.

He designed a solution, presented the concept to Goldman Sachs, and received direct product direction from their futures desk: "Needs long and short side. Build from public prices."

He built exactly that. The Yggdrasil Compute Index now tracks 56 GPU models across 28 providers with continuous price collection. He is seeking seed funding to take the product to market.
